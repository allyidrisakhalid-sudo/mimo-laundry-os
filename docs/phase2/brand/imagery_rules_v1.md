# Imagery Rules v1

## Objective

This document defines how imagery should be used in Mimo Phase 2. Imagery must build trust and warmth on public-facing pages without interfering with product clarity inside operational portals.

## Relationship to P2.0

P2.0 established a minimal, role-focused product structure.
Imagery in P2.1 must support that structure by:

* helping public pages feel human and trustworthy
* keeping internal product screens clean and task-oriented
* reinforcing cleanliness, professionalism, and care
* avoiding visual noise and redundancy

---

## 1. Primary Imagery Direction

### Locked Primary Choice

Use **real photography** as the primary imagery system.

### Secondary Support

Simple abstract shapes, color blocks, or subtle UI mockups may support layouts, but photography is the primary expression.

## Reason for choice

Real photography is the best fit for Mimo because it:

* builds trust faster
* makes the service feel real and local
* supports mass-market accessibility
* communicates cleanliness and professionalism better than illustration alone
* aligns with practical service expectations

## Rule

Do not create an illustration-first brand language for Phase 2.

---

## 2. Subject Rules

### Preferred Subjects

Show:

* clean folded clothes
* clean laundry in tidy baskets or organized surfaces
* happy but natural households
* confident, professional drivers
* organized handover moments
* clean hub/shop environments
* careful handling of laundry
* everyday trust and convenience

### Professionalism Rule

People shown should look:

* approachable
* neat
* respectful
* competent
* calm

Do not make staff look like fashion models or exaggerated ad actors.

---

## 3. Background Rules

### Preferred Background Style

* bright
* warm
* well-lit
* clean
* minimal clutter
* believable real environments

### Environment Guidance

Backgrounds should feel:

* practical
* tidy
* hygienic
* modern enough to feel premium
* local and relatable rather than generic luxury

---

## 4. Do / Don't Rules

## DO show

* cleanliness
* care
* order and organization
* calm confidence
* practical pickup and delivery moments
* trustworthy handling of garments
* tidy shop or home contexts
* professionalism in transport and service handoff

## DONT show

* messy laundry piles as the main image
* dirty or wrinkled before imagery dominating the page
* low-quality stock photos
* random smiling business handshakes
* unrealistic luxury mansion settings
* visually noisy backgrounds
* generic office stock imagery unrelated to laundry
* dramatic filters or over-edited color grading

---

## 5. Placement Rules

### Imagery should appear on:

* homepage hero
* homepage supporting sections if needed
* partners page hero/support sections
* help page header/support sections where trust or reassurance matters
* occasional signup/login supporting layout space if it improves warmth

### Imagery may appear in:

* testimonials or trust sections on public pages
* partner recruitment context
* simple brand blocks that need human warmth

### Imagery should NOT dominate:

* customer portal
* driver portal
* hub portal
* affiliate portal
* admin portal
* dev portal

## Portal Rule

Inside role portals, imagery should be rare or absent.
Operational screens should rely on layout, typography, data, and statusnot photography.

---

## 6. UI Mockup and Screenshot Rule

If product UI visuals are shown on public pages:

* they must reflect the real design system
* they must not invent fake pages outside the P2.0 screen map
* they must be simple and believable
* they should support trust, not overwhelm the hero

---

## 7. Quality Rules

All imagery used must be:

* sharp
* well-lit
* high-resolution
* color-consistent with the brand palette
* cropped intentionally
* free from obvious stock-photo clichés

---

## 8. Cultural and Market Fit Rule

Imagery should feel relevant to the Mimo market and audience.
Avoid visual choices that feel detached from local reality or too foreign to be relatable.
The brand should feel modern and aspirational without losing familiarity.

---

## 9. Minimalism Rule

Photography is support, not the product.
Imagery should create trust on public surfaces, then get out of the way.

## Design Lock

Real photography is locked as the primary imagery direction for Mimo Phase 2. Internal product portals remain imagery-light and operationally clean.
