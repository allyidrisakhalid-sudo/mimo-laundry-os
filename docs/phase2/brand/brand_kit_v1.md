# Brand Kit v1

## Objective

This document defines the brand promise, tone, messaging pillars, and core bilingual brand expressions for Mimo Phase 2. It extends the P2.0 experience blueprint into a single consistent brand voice that supports both public conversion and operational product clarity.

## Relationship to P2.0

This brand kit must reinforce the following P2.0 constraints:

* minimal, role-based product experience
* warm and simple copy
* no duplicate information
* clean and confidence-building user journeys
* operational clarity inside portals
* bilingual EN/SW readiness across all surfaces

---

## 1. Brand Promise

### Locked Brand Promise

Mimo makes laundry feel easy, reliable, and professionally handled from pickup to return.

### Brand Promise Rule

The brand promise must always communicate:

* ease
* reliability
* care
* visible process confidence

It must not sound:

* luxury-only
* overly technical
* corporate
* vague

## 3. 5-Second Value Proposition

### English

Mimo helps households and businesses get clean laundry fast, reliably, and without the usual stress.

### Kiswahili

Mimo husaidia familia na biashara kupata huduma ya nguo safi haraka, kwa uhakika, bila usumbufu wa kawaida.

## Value Proposition Rule

This statement must explain Mimo within five seconds.
It must work on:

* homepage hero
* signup support text
* partners page lead section
* app welcome states where relevant

It must not:

* list too many features
* use corporate phrases
* sound like a slogan instead of a clear explanation

---

## 4. Tone of Voice Rules

## Tone Attributes

The Mimo brand tone must be:

* warm
* welcoming
* simple
* confident
* respectful
* calm
* helpful

## Tone Do Rules

* use clear everyday language
* sound human, not robotic
* keep sentences short
* explain actions simply
* emphasize confidence and care
* make the service feel easy and trustworthy

## Tone Dont Rules

* do not use corporate jargon
* do not use startup buzzwords
* do not sound luxurious or elite
* do not sound cold or transactional
* do not overpromise
* do not use complex English when simpler English works

## Examples

### Good

* Schedule pickup
* Track your order
* Your laundry is ready
* Well take you to the right workspace
* Your next task is ready

### Bad

* Optimize your fabric lifecycle
* Unlock operational excellence
* Seamlessly orchestrate garment logistics
* Activate enterprise-grade textile workflows

### Pillar 2  Trust and Care

### Pillar 3  Speed Options

Customers can choose service speed that fits their needs.

### Pillar 4  Fair Pricing

Pricing should feel transparent, reasonable, and worth it.

## Message Pillar Rule

Public pages, campaigns, and key product entry points should map back to these pillars.
Do not introduce extra message pillars in Phase 2.

## 7. Brand Expression by Surface

### Public site

Brand voice may be slightly more expressive, but still simple and clear.

### Customer portal

Brand voice must remain warm and supportive.

### Driver / Hub / Affiliate / Admin portals

Brand voice must become more direct and operational, while still staying polite and human.

### Dev portal

Brand voice should be calm and literal, with less marketing language.

---

## 8. Brand Consistency Rules

* The brand must feel like one product across public and portal surfaces
* Marketing and operations must share the same visual language family
* Public pages may be more expressive, but never visually separate from product UI
* Tone must stay understandable in both EN and SW
* Any new messaging must align with this document and the P2.0 copy baseline

## Design Lock

This document is the single source of truth for Mimo Phase 2 voice and messaging. Any new brand expression must be reviewed against this file before implementation.

---

## 9. Progression Rule from P2.0 to P2.1

P2.0 defined what exists.
P2.1 defines how it looks and sounds.

That means:

* P2.0 screen maps remain authoritative
* P2.1 may style and unify those screens, but may not add new UX surface area
* P2.1 tokens must make dashboards calmer, not busier
* P2.1 messaging must reinforce role clarity, not introduce more copy
* P2.1 imagery must support public trust, not invade operational screens
* P2.1 icons must improve scanning, not create visual clutter

## Brand-to-product behavior rule

Every future design decision must pass this test:

1. Does it support the P2.0 experience blueprint?
2. Does it use the P2.1 brand system without inventing a new style?
3. Does it make the product easier to use?
4. Does it preserve calm, minimal, role-correct clarity?

If the answer to any is no, reject the design decision.
