# Imagery Rules  Mimo Phase 2

## 1) Imagery Role in the Brand

Imagery in Mimo exists to reinforce trust, care, cleanliness, premium quality, and operational confidence. It must support the interface, not overpower it.

## 2) Photography Direction

- real people and believable business settings
- clean garments, folded fabrics, baskets, shelves, delivery handoff moments, shop counters, mobile interaction moments
- premium but real, not fashion-editorial
- warm natural skin tones
- clean lighting
- visible service professionalism
- local realism preferred over imported stock fantasy
- no exaggerated luxury staging

## 3) Illustration Direction

- soft line-based or softly filled premium minimal illustrations
- rounded geometry
- fabric-inspired flow and motion
- clean, low-clutter scenes
- supportive only for empty states, onboarding, feature explanation, and public site accents
- no cartoon mascot
- no childish character style
- no busy storybook scenes

## 4) Abstract Visual Direction

- silk-wave inspired gradients
- soft layered glows
- curved shapes echoing folded fabric and flow
- dark-to-light premium section transitions
- restrained atmospheric overlays only

## 5) Composition Rules

- the UI always remains primary
- hero imagery must leave room for clear copy
- dashboard/product screens must avoid decorative image clutter
- empty-state illustrations must remain secondary to the CTA and message
- no gallery-like image overload
- one strong image is better than many weak ones

## 6) Color and Editing Rules

- imagery must harmonize with Midnight Silk palette
- avoid oversaturated reds/oranges unless operationally required
- avoid cold gray corporate stock processing
- preserve skin tone realism
- maintain soft contrast and premium clarity
- gradient overlays, if used, must be subtle and readable

## 7) Accessibility and Performance Rules

- all imagery must have alt text strategy
- critical information must never exist only inside an image
- decorative imagery must be removable without loss of meaning
- images must be optimized for web/mobile performance
- public hero/media assets must not slow core loading paths
- screenshots/mockups used publicly must remain legible on mobile

## 8) Imagery Do / Dont

### DO
- use believable operational scenes
- use clean, bright, premium-real visuals
- use illustrations to simplify, not decorate excessively
- use abstract gradients for atmosphere with restraint
- show product confidence and cleanliness

### DO NOT
- use cheesy laundry stock photos
- use cartoon mascots
- use overposed fashion-luxury images
- use image-heavy dashboards
- use conflicting illustration styles
- use heavy blur behind essential text
- use photo treatments that reduce trust or clarity

## 9) Imagery Lock Statement

### P2.1 Imagery Lock

All future Phase 2 imagery must follow these rules and remain subordinate to usability and brand trust.
