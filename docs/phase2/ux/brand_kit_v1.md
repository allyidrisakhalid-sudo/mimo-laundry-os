# Brand Kit V1  Mimo Phase 2

## 1) Brand Position

Mimo is a premium, modern, trustworthy laundry platform that makes pickup, care, processing, tracking, and return feel simple and under control for everyday users and operators.

## 2) Brand Promise

Mimo gives customers and business operators a calm, clear, premium laundry experience with visible progress, reliable handoffs, and no confusion.

## 3) Brand Personality

Locked brand traits:
- warm
- polished
- calm
- trustworthy
- simple
- modern
- premium
- helpful
- clear
- disciplined

Traits not allowed:
- playful
- loud
- overly corporate
- slang-heavy
- luxury-fashion dramatic
- harsh enterprise
- startup-cute
- overly technical

## 4) Tone of Voice

Mimo uses:
- short sentences
- easy words
- welcoming but not casual
- premium but not elite
- direct but not cold
- helpful without over-explaining
- suitable for English and Swahili parity
- suitable for customers, drivers, affiliate staff, hub staff, admins, and partners
- suitable for mass-market Tanzanian use without sounding cheap

Tone behavior rules:
- Prefer plain clarity over cleverness.
- Keep instructions short and complete.
- Make the next step obvious.
- Use calm confidence, not hype.
- Avoid decorative phrasing in operational UI.
- Keep persuasion warmer on public pages and tighter inside portals.

## 5) Messaging Pillars

### 1. Trust and care
Your laundry is handled with care and tracked clearly.

### 2. Simplicity and control
Every step is easy to follow, with one clear next action.

### 3. Professional reliability
The service feels organized, visible, and dependable.

### 4. Premium everyday quality
The product looks refined and smooth without becoming intimidating.

## 6) Audience Translation Rules

### Public visitor
- primary emotional need: trust and curiosity
- copy emphasis: what Mimo does, how it works, why it feels reliable
- sentence style: warm, short, lightly persuasive
- words to avoid: optimize, infrastructure, orchestration, enterprise, stack

### Customer
- primary emotional need: reassurance and clarity
- copy emphasis: progress visibility, pickup/return confidence, clean next actions
- sentence style: simple, calm, reassuring
- words to avoid: incident, exception state, internal processing, escalation matrix

### Driver
- primary emotional need: speed and certainty
- copy emphasis: what to do now, where to go, what proof is required
- sentence style: direct, short, action-first
- words to avoid: explore, discover, maybe, flexible, delightful

### Hub Staff
- primary emotional need: control and queue clarity
- copy emphasis: stage visibility, task status, handoff certainty, operational order
- sentence style: concise, structured, operational
- words to avoid: whimsical, luxury, journey, magic, inspirational

### Affiliate Staff
- primary emotional need: ease and transaction confidence
- copy emphasis: fast order capture, correct handoff, confident customer-facing actions
- sentence style: clear, practical, supportive
- words to avoid: backend, system job, synchronization, workflow engine

### Affiliate Admin
- primary emotional need: shop performance visibility
- copy emphasis: shop activity, payouts, performance signals, issue visibility
- sentence style: calm, concise, commercially aware
- words to avoid: speculative, vague, poetic, engineering-heavy language

### Admin
- primary emotional need: oversight and intervention clarity
- copy emphasis: visibility, risk, control, reporting, decision actions
- sentence style: exact, concise, controlled
- words to avoid: playful wording, decorative slogans, fluffy reassurance

### DevAdmin
- primary emotional need: precision and caution
- copy emphasis: explicit impact, exact states, safe overrides, traceability
- sentence style: precise, minimal, caution-first
- words to avoid: friendly ambiguity, marketing language, soft claims without specifics

## 7) Copy Style Guardrails

- no paragraph walls
- no decorative slogans inside operational UI
- no duplicate CTA labels for different meanings
- no raw technical jargon in user-facing screens
- no ambiguous action words like Proceed where Create order or Open task is clearer
- every screen title must be plain and exact
- every CTA must describe the action clearly
- every empty state must reassure and direct the next step

Additional guardrails:
- Use one primary action per view where possible.
- Use labels that match the result of the action.
- Keep status language consistent across roles.
- Keep EN and SW meaning aligned, not loosely approximated.

## 8) Brand Do / Do Not

### DO
- sound calm
- sound premium
- sound simple
- sound trustworthy
- keep instructions short
- keep labels clean
- reinforce clarity and visibility

### DO NOT
- use slang
- use all-caps marketing claims
- sound playful or childish
- sound like a bank/legal notice unless necessary
- overuse smart, best, revolutionary, or AI
- write decorative luxury poetry
- create fear in error messages

## 9) Front-End Application Rules

Tone shifts by surface:
- public pages: warmer and more persuasive
- customer app: reassuring and simple
- driver app: direct and action-first
- hub and affiliate portals: operational and clean
- admin and dev portals: concise and controlled

Application rules:
- Public pages may sell confidence, but never overpromise.
- Customer surfaces must reduce uncertainty at every step.
- Driver surfaces must privilege speed, proof, and clarity.
- Hub and affiliate surfaces must keep queue work obvious.
- Admin and dev surfaces must stay explicit, low-noise, and controlled.

## P2.1 Brand Lock

All Phase 2 pages, components, and microcopy must follow this brand kit with no deviations.
